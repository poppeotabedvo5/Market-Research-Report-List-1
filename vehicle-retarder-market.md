<p><p>The vehicle retarder market is primarily categorized into electric and hydraulic retarders. Electric retarders use electrical energy to create resistance, providing efficient speed control and reduced wear on traditional braking systems. They are often favored in electric and hybrid vehicles for their lightweight design and lower maintenance needs. In contrast, hydraulic retarders utilize fluid dynamics for braking force, offering robust performance in heavy-duty applications like trucks and buses. Both technologies enhance safety and extend the lifespan of braking components in vehicles.</p></p>
